Things to do in Iceland

The Aurora Borealis makes its name from the Roman goddess of dawn,Aurom,and the Greek name for the month nrth wind, Boreans. From september to april, Iceland is a hotspot for this magnificient light show.

The Aurora Borealis, more commonly referred to as the Northern lights is a natural phenomenon created when particles emitted by the sun interact with the atmosphere in the earths magnetic field.This releases energy, causing peculiar luminious green streaks across the skies.

When making a trip to Iceland, it is not to pay special attention to the country's namescale-namely, its 4,500 square miles of glacier. Ice climbing on the glaciers is practiced year-round and takes place mainly on the Sothemajokull and Svinafellsjokull glaciers in the south of Iceland, to which day trips are offered from Reykjavik (and Skafiafell).
